Description

Banh Mi is a favorite street food item in Vietnamese culture. Banh Mi just means bread and it can be combined with various meats. One of the more popular type is a Grilled Pork Banh Mi. The pork is marinated in lemongrass, fish sauce and other ingredients. Combined with pickled vegetables and a french baguette this combination is a cheap and delicious street food staple.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1/4 soy sauce
  • 4 cloves Garlic; minced
  • 2 stalks Lemongrass; finely chopped. Lemongrass paste is a fine substitute
  • 1/4 cup of Canola Oil
  • 1 tablespoon Black Pepper
  • 1 whole lime; juiced
  • 1 pound pork butt or pre cut pork chops ; cut into 1/4 inch thick slices as long s the butt is wide
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup cider vinegar
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/2 pounds carrots; julienned
  • 1/2 pounds daikon/jicama; julienned
  • 8 french baguettes
Optional Garnishes
  • 1/4 cup Kewpie mayonnaise; or regular mayo
  • Cilantro and basil
  • sliced cucumber
  • Jalepenos
  • Hoison Sauce
  • Sriracha Sauce

Steps

Prep:
  1. Combine the fish sauce, soy sauce garlic, lemongrass, canola oil, pepper and lime juice in a bowl and whisk to blend.
  2. Pour the marinade over the pork butt slices in a large bag and refrigerate overnight.
  3. Combine water, vinegar and sugar in bowl and mix to dissolve the sugar
  4. Add the carrots, cucumbers and daikon/jicama and chill overnight
Cooking
  1. Grill the pork till its just pink in the center, internal temp of 160Â°F
  2. Remove from grill and cut the strips into smaller bite sized pieces
  3. Grill the rolls for a minute to add a nice crust on the inside
Finish
  1. Slather the rolls with a 1/2 teaspoon of mayo
  2. Spread some hoisin sauce over the mayo on both sides of the roll
  3. Add the pork
  4. Add the pickled carrot,cucumber and daikon/jicama mix
  5. Add optional garnishes
